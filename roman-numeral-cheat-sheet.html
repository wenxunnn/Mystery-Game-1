Reading rule: symbols are normally added left to right (VI = 5 + 1 = 6). If a smaller symbol comes before a larger one, it's subtracted instead (IV = 5 − 1 = 4). Only certain smaller-before-larger pairs are valid: IV, IX, XL, XC, CD, CM.

A block of X's adds 10 for each X (XX = 20, XXX = 30, and so on). Tack VII (+7) or IX (+9) onto the end of a block of X's to get the full number.

Careful: VII is three characters long; IX is only two. Count the blanks in any cipher carefully — the total length tells you how many X's are hiding in front.
