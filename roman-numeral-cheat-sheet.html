Reading rule: Roman numerals are usually added from left to right. For example, VI = 5 + 1 = 6. If a smaller symbol appears before a larger one, subtract it instead. For example, IV = 4 and IX = 9.

Building Larger Numbers

X + VII = XVII
XX + VII = XXVII
XXX + VII = XXXVII
X + IX = XIX
XX + IX = XXIX
XXX + IX = XXXIX

A block of X's adds 10 for each X. After the block of X's, another ending can be attached. For example, VII adds 7 and IX adds 9.

Careful: VII has three characters, while IX has two characters. In a cipher with blanks, the number of blanks may matter.
